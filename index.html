What's Typers?

Basically, Typers will manage the fonts you depends on. Using the Google Fonts as the main registry, now you have the ability to deal with the typography your app uses with no hassle of accessing the website, copying and pasting the link of the font you picked.

A better comprehension

Today, the front-end has a rock-solid community and support environment. Your dependencies can be easily managed by Bower, for instance.

In Node.js territory, you can handle the management of your dependencies with npm.

Based on these facilities, Typers has born – to manage your fonts on demand, with ease, wisdom and focusing productivity.

About "Typers" name

When thinking about the project's name, we thought in something similar to fonts or typography.

At the beginning, we thought about fontsjs, fower and fonwer (yes, inspired by Bower). But due to the unavailability of the name, we had to go further.

Then, some sketches here and there until we found Typers. It is simple, striking and reminds what all of us are: typers.

We are just getting started

Typers is still in alpha and has bugs and lots of missing features. There are amazing things to come but there are all in roadmap, nothing solid on code.

Based on these facts, keep in mind Typers won't solve all the problems of the world. Initially, it will keep simple to manage fonts with few rules and no bureaucracies.

Installation

Requirements

The unique hard requirement is npm.

Open your terminal or command prompt and shout: 

npm install -g typers

Then confirm if Typers is really installed by running: 

typers -v
